## 🧠 Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between **cryptocurrency trader performance** and **market sentiment (Fear & Greed Index)**.

The goal is to uncover behavioral patterns, trading inefficiencies, and insights that can contribute to **data-driven trading strategies**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze how **market sentiment impacts trader profitability**
* Identify **behavioral patterns** (fear-driven trading, overtrading, etc.)
* Compare **top traders vs average traders**
* Extract **actionable insights for smarter trading strategies**

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

This project uses two primary datasets:

### 1. 📈 Historical Data (`Historical_data.csv`)

Contains trade-level data from Hyperliquid:

* Account
* Execution Price
* Trade Size (USD & Tokens)
* Side (Buy/Sell)
* Closed PnL
* Fees
* Timestamp

---

### 2. 📊 Market Sentiment (`fear_greed_index.csv`)

Daily Bitcoin sentiment classification:

* Fear
* Extreme Fear
* Neutral
* Greed
* Extreme Greed

## 🔍 Key Insights

* 📉 Traders exhibit **low win rates**, indicating inefficient strategies
* 😨 **Trading activity peaks during Fear**, not Greed
* 💸 Higher activity leads to **increased fees and reduced net profitability**
* 📊 Profits during Fear are driven by **few large trades (high volatility)**
* 🧠 Traders show a **"buy-the-dip" bias**, often unsuccessfully
* 🏆 Top traders demonstrate **more consistent and disciplined behavior**

Understanding these patterns can help in designing **more disciplined and profitable trading strategies**.
